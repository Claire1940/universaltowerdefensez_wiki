<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Unit Optimization Flow">

To optimize a newly acquired meta unit, follow a strict sequence of upgrades. Rerolling traits before leveling a unit or confirming its role in your team composition often leads to wasted resources.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Secure the Unit and Confirm Role">
    Before spending any upgrade materials, ensure the unit fits a specific gap in your current team. Identify whether they function as a Main DPS, AOE Clear, Support Buffer, or Economy Farm unit.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Level Up via Normal Progression">
    Bring the unit to its maximum level using standard gameplay or XP items. Leveling increases base stats, giving you a clearer picture of how much value a percentage-based Trait or Stat roll will actually add.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Apply Trait Rerolls">
    Use your saved Trait Rerolls from active codes and challenges. Aim for high-impact offensive traits on damage dealers, and utility or cooldown reduction traits on support characters.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Lock and Optimize Stats">
    Once a desirable trait is secured, use Stat Rerolls to fine-tune individual stat lines. Apply Stat Locks to protect high-tier rolls (such as Crit Damage or Attack Speed) while rolling for the remaining slots.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Evolve and Etherealize">
    Gather specific evolution materials from Raids and Challenges. Evolving the unit unlocks their final form, significantly boosting base multipliers and skill ranges.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Resource Management Warning">
Stat Locks are exceptionally rare. Do not use them on mid-tier units. Save them exclusively for S+ tier carries like Joyful Captain and King Sailor (Unrivaled).
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Evolution & Etherealization Priorities">

Evolution completely transforms a unit's capabilities, often adding massive AOE coverage, status effects (like freeze or burn), or team-wide stat buffs. Etherealization acts as a secondary progression system that provides flat multiplier boosts to your character's base kit.

Use the following priority list when deciding which units to evolve first:

1. **Primary Carry Evolving**: Evolve your main damage dealer first. A single S+ evolved carry can solo most mid-game content.
2. **Economy Evolving**: Evolve your farm units next to lower their placement costs and increase wave-by-wave income.
3. **Support Evolving**: Evolve buffers last, as their primary value comes from percentage increases which scale better once your carries are already maxed out.

| Unit Form | Base Damage | Evolved Damage | Added Utility Upon Evolution |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Basic Carry** | 100% | 250% | Basic single-target attacks |
| **Evolved Carry** | 250% | 500%+ | Massive AOE, Burn/Bleed effects, Stun |
| **Basic Support** | 100% | 150% | Small single-target range buff |
| **Evolved Support** | 150% | 300% | Map-wide buffs, Cooldown reduction aura |

<Callout type="warning" title="Evolution Warning">
Do not evolve units randomly just because you have the materials. Save your fragments for top-tier meta units. Check the active update patch notes to ensure your target unit hasn't been nerfed.
</Callout>
